What is QUNET?

QUNET is a research-oriented initiative exploring the future of secure communication beyond classical networking models. It focuses on conceptual architectures inspired by quantum networking, trust propagation, and next-generation security principles.

Future of the Quantum Internet

Quantum networking concepts rely on physical principles rather than computational assumptions, enabling fundamentally new security models.

Quantum internet: How It Works

Information exchange is supported by entanglement and photon-based signaling, reducing reliance on centralized trust authorities.

Why It Is Secure

Quantum-inspired security models rely on physical constraints, making unauthorized interception inherently detectable.

Research Architecture (QUNET ‚Äì LIPAS ‚Äì QUP)

QUNET defines the overarching research framework. LIPAS explores personalized, liveness-based authorization at the identity layer. QUP investigates how verified trust signals could propagate across future secure infrastructures.

How LIPAS Works

LIPAS evaluates authorization through real-time liveness verification, physiological signals, and environmental integrity checks. Authorization is bound to the moment of interaction and cannot be reused.

Why LIPAS Is Different

Unlike credential-based systems, LIPAS does not store identities, secrets, or reusable authorization artifacts. Trust exists only during active verification.

Threat Model (LIPAS)

LIPAS assumes compromised networks, hostile environments, and advanced threat actors. Any anomaly immediately halts authorization without escalation.

Attacks result in interruption ‚Äî not compromise.
